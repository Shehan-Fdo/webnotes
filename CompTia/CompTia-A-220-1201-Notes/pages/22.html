Ethernet Wiring Standards

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 3.2

International and National Standards

  • Ethernet wiring standards are globally recognized and defined by international and national organizations.
  • The ISO/IEC 11801 standard is an international cabling standard that defines different classes of networking standards.
  • In the US, the Telecommunications Industry Association (TIA) manages the ANSI/TIA-568 standard for commercial building cabling installations.
  • The ANSI/TIA-568 standard specifies wire color assignments for Ethernet cables using RJ45 connectors.

T568A and T568B Wiring Schemes

  • The T568A and T568B are the two primary color-coding schemes for Ethernet cables.
  • Both schemes use the same RJ45 connector but differ in the arrangement of wire colors on specific pins.
  • T568B is the most commonly used standard in the US and many other regions.
  • Organizations typically choose one standard and maintain consistency throughout their installations.
  • Mixing T568A and T568B on opposite ends of a cable is not recommended, especially for gigabit networks, as it can cause connectivity issues.

Color Code Comparison

  • Both T568A and T568B share identical color assignments for some pins, simplifying memorization.
  • Common colors for both schemes:
    • Pin 4: Blue
    • Pin 5: White/Blue
    • Pin 7: White/Brown
    • Pin 8: Brown
  • Differences between T568A and T568B:
    • T568A:
      • Pin 1: White/Green
      • Pin 2: Green
      • Pin 3: White/Orange
      • Pin 6: Orange
    • T568B:
      • Pin 1: White/Orange
      • Pin 2: Orange
      • Pin 3: White/Green
      • Pin 6: Green
  • To identify the wiring scheme, examine the colors on pins 1, 2, 3, and 6.

Practical Applications and Best Practices

  • Understanding these standards is essential for cable installation, troubleshooting, and certification exams like the A+.
  • When crimping cables, verify the color order before securing the connector to avoid errors.
  • Some keystone jacks and wall plates provide color-coded guides for both T568A and T568B, simplifying installation.
  • Example of a keystone jack layout:
    • T568A: Green, White/Green, Blue, White/Blue on one side; Orange, White/Orange, White/Brown, Brown on the other.
    • T568B: Orange, White/Orange, Blue, White/Blue on one side; Green, White/Green, White/Brown, Brown on the other.
